About Me

I received M.E. Degree and Ph.D. in Information and Computer Science from Osaka University in 2005 and 2008, respectively. I was a visiting scholar in University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I was a research fellow of the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) from April 2007 to March 2009. I am an Assistant Professor in Mobile Computing Laboratory in Department of Information Networking, Graduate School of Information Science and Technology, Osaka University. I am a member of Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE), IEEE Computer Society (IEEE-CS), Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), Institute of Electronics, Information and Communication Engineers (IEICE), and Information Processing Society of Japan (IPSJ).

My primary research interests are in the areas of:

  • Mobile Sensing and Computing,
  • Context Recognition,
  • Healthcare.
